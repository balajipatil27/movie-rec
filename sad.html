Coco released : 2017
Anime
"Coco" is a 2017 animated musical film produced by Pixar Animation Studios and released by Walt Disney Pictures. Directed by Lee Unkrich and co-directed by Adrian Molina, the story revolves around a young boy named Miguel Rivera who dreams of becoming a musician, despite his family's generations-old ban on music. On the Day of the Dead, Miguel accidentally finds himself in the Land of the Dead, where he encounters his deceased relatives and embarks on a journey to uncover his family's history and understand the true meaning behind their prohibition of music. Filled with vibrant animation, heartfelt storytelling, and memorable music, "Coco" celebrates Mexican culture and traditions while exploring themes of family, legacy, and following one's dreams. The film received widespread critical acclaim and won several awards, including the Academy Award for Best Animated Feature.

A charlie Brown Christmas released : 1965
"A Charlie Brown Christmas" is a beloved animated television special that premiered on December 9, 1965. Produced by Lee Mendelson and directed by Bill Melendez, the special is based on the comic strip "Peanuts" by Charles M. Schulz. The story follows the iconic character Charlie Brown as he navigates the commercialization of Christmas and struggles to find the true meaning of the holiday. Feeling disillusioned by the materialism around him, Charlie Brown seeks guidance from his friends, including the philosophical Linus and the spirited Snoopy. Along the way, they work together to produce a Christmas play and discover the importance of friendship, kindness, and the simple joys of the season. "A Charlie Brown Christmas" is celebrated for its endearing characters, timeless themes, memorable soundtrack by Vince Guaraldi, and charming hand-drawn animation. It has become a holiday classic cherished by audiences of all ages.

Soul released :2020
"Soul" is a 2020 animated film produced by Pixar Animation Studios and released by Walt Disney Pictures. Directed by Pete Docter and co-directed by Kemp Powers, the film tells the story of Joe Gardner, a middle-school music teacher and aspiring jazz musician who embarks on an extraordinary journey to discover the true essence of life and what it means to have a soul. After a near-death experience, Joe finds himself in the "Great Before," a realm where souls develop their personalities before being born. There, he encounters a precocious soul named 22, who has yet to find her passion for life. Together, Joe and 22 embark on a quest through the realms of existence, learning valuable lessons about purpose, passion, and the interconnectedness of all living beings. "Soul" received widespread acclaim for its thought-provoking storytelling, stunning animation, and rich exploration of existential themes. It won the Academy Award for Best Animated Feature, among numerous other accolades.

Tarzan released 1999
ANIMAL
"Tarzan" is a 1999 animated film produced by Walt Disney Feature Animation and released by Walt Disney Pictures. Directed by Chris Buck and Kevin Lima, the film is based on the story "Tarzan of the Apes" by Edgar Rice Burroughs. It follows the life of Tarzan, a young man raised by gorillas in the African jungle after his parents are killed by a leopard. As Tarzan grows up, he struggles to find his place between his human origins and his adopted gorilla family. When a group of explorers, including the adventurous Jane Porter, arrives in the jungle, Tarzan discovers his human heritage and faces conflicts between his loyalties. Filled with memorable characters, lush animation, and an iconic soundtrack by Phil Collins, "Tarzan" is celebrated for its action-packed adventure, heartfelt storytelling, and themes of identity, acceptance, and love.
